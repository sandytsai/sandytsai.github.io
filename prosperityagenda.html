The Prosperity Agenda

Design Like Mad is an event hosted by Adobe, where designers work on a project for a nonprofit in a day. During this event, I teamed up with other designers to redesign Money Powerup Packs for the Prosperity Agenda. “Money Powerup Packs” are kits with informational documents and activities used for financial education, sent to the staff at centers for lower-income families. The materials are printed and assembled by TPA staff, and the boxes contain many loose sheets of paper, with no clear hierarchy of importance nor guidance in terms of where to start.

Client

The Prosperity Agenda

Team

Chad Peltola (Art Director), Madison Zeller (UX Designer), Tess Li (UX Designer)

Process

10 hours to research, ideate, sketch, and design

The Challenge

Help TPA redesign these kits – both the packaging itself as well as the included materials – to improve the overall user experience. We focused on one kit with the assumption that our design solution can be applied to the other 7 and any future kits they may create.

Original Design

Our Strategy

With only 10 hours to tackle this challenge, we started by understanding the problem, identifying the target users and the main pain points in the current design. Then, we created a strategy to target three aspects that needed redesign. After dividing our work into these three parts, we figured out how to organize the content using post-its and sketched new designs.

Packaging

Currently, the box is too big, and the materials are loose and separate. Moreover, the organization offers no direction of where to start and how the materials relate to one another.

Branding

The branding needed a cleaner and modern look that would be inviting and friendly. Facilitators need to feel empowered and families need to feel supported and excited about the event.

Content Layout

The content layout needed to be user friendly and offer clear instructions to both the facilitators and families. The visual design needed an update to show more hierarchy of information.

Target Users

Facilitator

"I need the support and directions to help the parents of low-income families feel educated and informed about financial management."

Father

"I want to be better prepared to take care of my family and educate them financially."

Process

Solution

We decreased the box size and broke up the content into clearly labelled sections to eliminate free floating content and confusion. Everything now has a clear relationship and purpose. The USB of digital content is now taped onto the box with directions. To add an introduction and guide, we created a roadmap that explains what this box is for and the three main steps to complete. I was responsible for designing the roadmap. We also started to redesign the content layout and branding.

What I learned

This was project was a great exercise to analyze how users perceive information. Designing the roadmap taught me how to provide users with a clearer mental model in an experience. I also learned about the tangible considerations that would affect user experience, such as how the box’s content should look like when it’s first opened, and how the size of the box should contain the contents.

Further Development

I would work on redesigning both the content layout and the content itself. I would like to go beyond the packaging of a single pack and go deeper into the entire user experience of the Power Up Packs program. I would also strengthen the visual and packaging design to make the kit even more delightful to own, so that facilitators and families get more excited about its content.
