Origami Cupcake Liners

At Chronicle Books, the creative director and industrial designer asked the design fellows to design and pitch concept ideas for a product that could be a "clever container." As an individual project, I came up with the idea of a origami cupcake liner kit and presented the editors with a prototype. The kit contains 12 patterned square sheets of paper and origami instructions for making square cupcake liners.

Client

Chronicle Books

Process

3 weeks for research, ideation, design, mockups

Tools

Illustrator, Photoshop, digital camera, x-acto knife, cutting mat, cake, paper

incorporating interaction

I wanted to create a product that users can interact with, not just use as a container. I researched materials extensively, both for the container itself and what could be contained. Paper is a versatile material that is easy to handle, has great interactive potential, and has a refined quality. Although I could easily design any origami container, I wanted to take a step further and make the experience even more unique.

solution

I researched different origami techniques to see what could be contained in them. As Chronicle Books is well known for its whimsical cookbooks and playful gifts, I thought it would be fitting for the Chronicle Books brand to use origami as a cupcake container and create a DIY kit out of it. The concept combines baking and packaging with more user interaction. The liners provide bakers with a packaging material to make their cupcakes more beautiful, while the origami kit also provides a fun experience that makes their cupcakes feel more personal.

process

To implement this idea, I found origami instructions that would fit this purpose, and designed my own set of instructions along with the packaging for the kit liners. I designed the liner patterns and created guidelines to make it easier to fold. For the cover of the kit, I used my liners and some cake to take photos of what the end product would look like.
Instructions are provided on the interior of the kit packaging to make it more durable and harder to lose.
Liners have guidelines to make it easier for users to follow the instructions.
